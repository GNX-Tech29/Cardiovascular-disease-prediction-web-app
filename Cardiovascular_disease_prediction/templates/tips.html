Heart Health Matters !

Make Your Heart Happy

Prevention Tips

Exercise regularly: Regular exercise helps to lower blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels. It also helps to maintain a healthy weight

Maintain a healthy weight :Being overweight or obese is a major risk factor for CVD. If you are overweight or obese, losing even a small amount of weight can help to lower your risk.

Don't smoke: Smoking is one of the leading risk factors for CVD. If you smoke, quitting is the single best thing you can do for your heart health.

Get regular checkups: See your doctor for regular checkups, including blood pressure checks and cholesterol screenings

Improving Cardiovascular Health

Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables: Fruits and vegetables are low in saturated fat, trans fat, and cholesterol, and they are high in fiber. They are also a good source of vitamins and minerals that are important for heart health

Choose lean protein sources: Lean protein sources, such as fish, chicken, and beans, are low in saturated fat and cholesterol. They are also a good source of protein, which is essential for maintaining a healthy weight.

Limit saturated and trans fats: Saturated and trans fats can raise cholesterol levels, which can increase your risk of CVD. Limit your intake of saturated fat to less than 10% of your total calories, and limit your intake of trans fat to as close to zero as possible.

Choose healthy fats: Healthy fats, such as those found in olive oil, avocados, and nuts, can help to lower cholesterol levels and protect your heart health.

Get enough fiber: Fiber helps to lower cholesterol levels and keep you feeling full. Aim to eat 25-30 grams of fiber per day.

Drink plenty of water: Water helps to keep your blood pressure and blood sugar levels in check. Aim to drink 8 glasses of water per day.

Get enough sleep: Sleep is important for overall health, including heart health. Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep per night
